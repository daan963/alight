French footballer Karim Benzema and Interior Minister Gerald DarmaninAFP

Karim Benzema has filed a defamation case against French Interior Minister Gerald Darmanin. Darmanin accused the 35-year-old French footballer of having ties to the Muslim Brotherhood when he condemned Israel’s attacks on Palestinians last October.

French news agency AFP reported that Benzema’s lawyer, Hugo Vigier, filed the case in the country’s special Court of Justice on Tuesday. In this court the proceedings of the cases of the ministers of France were completed.

After the Palestinian independence armed group Hamas attacked Israel on October 7, Israel began indiscriminate bombing of Gaza in response. When there was a storm of protest in different parts of the world, Benzema also joined in it. The Algerian-born footballer wrote on social media, “All our prayers are with the residents of Gaza, who are once again the victims of this unjust bombing, where no woman or child is spared.”

France’s Interior Minister Darmanin told CNews Channel that the Ballon d’Or winner Benzema has “notorious ties to the Muslim Brotherhood.” The Muslim Brotherhood is a Cairo-based Islamic organization that is currently banned in Egypt. However, the branches of the organization are spread all over the world. Darmanin also accused Benzema of not singing the national anthem at France national team games and proselytizing on social media.Read moreIsraeli footballer arrested for preaching war with Palestine freed

Benzema protested through his lawyer shortly after Darmanin’s latest allegations. This time he walked the legal path. In the lawsuit, Benzema said, “He has never had the slightest involvement with the Muslim Brotherhood, and has no idea of ​​anyone claiming to be a member of the organization.”

Karim Benzema now plays for Saudi Pro League side Al IttihadAFP

The record for the most sixes in an innings in T20 International was held by Hazratullah Zazai so far. The Afghanistan opener hit 16 sixes en route to a 162-run innings against Ireland in Dehradun in 2019.

Allen’s 137 is also New Zealand’s individual highest innings in T20 Internationals. The Kiwi opener broke Brendon McCullum’s record. McCullum scored 123 off 58 balls against Bangladesh at Pallekele in the 2012 T20 World Cup.
