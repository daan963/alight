History of the World Darts Championship

Dennis Priestley to Michael Smith: A Legacy of Champions

The roots of the World Darts Championship trace back to 1994, with Dennis Priestley claiming the inaugural title. Phil Taylor then dominated the scene, securing a staggering fourteen titles between 1995 and 2013. However, new champions emerged, including Michael van Gerwen, Gary Anderson, and the defending champion, Michael Smith.

The Spectacle of Nine Darters

A highlight of the championship is the elusive nine-darter, a perfect leg. From Raymond van Barneveld’s historic moment in 2009 to Michael Smith’s recent feat in 2023, we explore the mesmerizing instances of perfection that have graced Alexandra Palace.

Favorites for the World Title

Luke Humphries: The Rising Star

In a surprising turn of events, Luke Humphries has emerged as the top favorite for the world title. With three major titles in just 49 days, including the World Grand Prix and Players Championship Finals, can “Cool Hand Luke” carry this exceptional form to secure his first world title?

While Michael van Gerwen and Gerwyn Price aim for a resurgence, defending champion Michael Smith remains a wildcard. Prize Money Breakdown

A Whopping 2.5 Million in Prize Money

The allure of the World Darts Championship isn’t just about the glory; it’s also about the substantial prize pool. The 2024 edition boasts a staggering £2.5 million, a testament to the growing prestige of the championship. Compare this to the inaugural edition in 1994, where the entire prize pool was a mere £64,000.
