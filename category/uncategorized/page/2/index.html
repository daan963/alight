Everything You Need Know About World Darts Championship 2024

December 12, 2023 by Herbert

The world of darts is set to ignite once again as Michael Smith defends his title against the finest players on the planet at the iconic Alexandra Palace. Let’s dive into the heart of the action and explore the ins and outs of the World Darts Championship 2024, promising drama, fun, and entertainment.

The Rich History of the World Darts Championship

The championship’s journey since the inaugural PDC World Championship began 30 years ago has been a rollercoaster of iconic walk-ons, on-stage arguments, monumental upsets, and the magic of nine-darters.

From the unforgettable “I can’t spake” incident to nail-biting matches, the World Championship has consistently delivered unforgettable moments that have etched themselves into the annals of darting history.

Where and Where World Darts Championship 2024

The 31st World Darts Championship kicks off on December 15, 2023, and concludes on January 3, 2024. Action-packed days unfold, excluding Christmas Eve, Christmas Day, Boxing Day, and New Year’s Eve.

Mark your calendar for the kickoff on December 15 at 7pm, with afternoon sessions at 12.30pm and evening sessions at 7pm continuing until the quarter-finals on January 1. The semi-finals follow on January 2, and the grand finale unfolds on January 3 at 7pm.

Since 2008, the Alexandra Palace in London has been the revered host of the championship. What makes this venue special? Let’s unravel the charm that draws fans year after year.

Event Format

The World Championship employs a sets format, with each set requiring the first to secure three legs. The deciding set introduces a twist, necessitating a two-leg lead for victory.

As the championship progresses, the required sets for victory increase: 3 sets for rounds 1 and 2, 4 sets for rounds 3 and 4, 5 sets for the quarter-finals, 6 sets for the semi-finals, and 7 sets for the final showdown.

The Legacy of Phil Taylor

The Dominance of ‘Mighty Mike’

The subsequent years witnessed other formidable players like Peter Wright, Gerwyn Price, and Michael Smith stepping into the limelight.

2024 World Darts Championship Preview: What to Expect in the First Round for Dutch Players?

December 9, 2023 by Herbert

The excitement is palpable as we approach the commencement of the 2024 World Darts Championship on December 15. In this exclusive preview, we delve into the prospects of the Dutch contenders who are set to grace the stage during the first round. With twelve Dutch participants in Alexandra Palace, the spotlight is on eight who kick off their campaign from the initial rounds. Let’s explore the matchups, storylines, and potential upsets that could unfold in the opening clashes.

Kevin Doets: A Debut with High Stakes (Opening Match – Dec. 15)

Kevin Doets, riding on the momentum of his recent PDC ranking final at Players Championship 20, faces the challenge of securing his Tour Card in his debut at Alexandra Palace. His opponent, Stowe Buntz, the CDC Continental Cup winner, poses a formidable threat, setting the stage for a gripping showdown.

Niels Zonneveld’s Redemption Quest (Evening Session – Dec. 17)

Niels Zonneveld, after two previous attempts, aims for redemption at Alexandra Palace. Having showcased remarkable form at the Players Championship Finals, Zonneveld sets his sights on overcoming former quarterfinalist Darren Webster in a clash that promises intense darts action.

Jermaine Wattimena vs. Fallon Sherrock (Evening Session – Dec. 17)

Jermaine Wattimena, a seasoned participant, enters the World Darts Championship for the tenth consecutive time. His opponent, Fallon Sherrock, a rising star, adds intrigue to the matchup. This encounter on Dec. 17 is a collision of experience and emerging talent, making it a must-watch duel.

Gian van Veen’s Stellar Debut (Evening Session – Dec. 18)

Gian van Veen, enjoying a stellar first year as a Tour Card holder, faces off against Hong Kong’s Man-Lok Leung. With impressive achievements throughout the year, both players bring a unique dynamic to the Alexandra Palace, promising an exhilarating match on December 18.

Wessel Nijman’s Debut Challenge (Evening Session – Dec. 19)

Wessel Nijman, benefiting from his success on the Development Tour, makes his debut at Alexandra Palace. His opponent, the experienced Steve Beaton, participates in the PDC World Championship for a record-extending 23rd time. Will Nijman’s debut be memorable, or will Beaton’s experience prevail?

Richard Veenstra’s Alexandra Palace Debut (Evening Session – Dec. 20)

Richard Veenstra, after securing his Tour Card, enters the Alexandra Palace with a strong season behind him. His opponent, New Zealander Ben Robb, adds an international flavor to the matchup. Can Veenstra continue his impressive form against the Kiwi contender on December 20?

Christian Kist vs. Luke Littler (Evening Session – Dec. 20)

Christian Kist, a former Lakeside winner, faces off against 16-year-old debutant Luke Littler. Kist, despite suffering an injury at the Players Championship Finals, brings experience, while Littler carries the enthusiasm of youth. Will it be a passing of the torch or a veteran’s triumph on December 20?

Luke Woodhouse vs. Berry van Peer (Afternoon Session – Dec. 21)

In an afternoon clash on December 21, Berry van Peer, propelled by four Challenge Tour titles, makes his debut at the World Darts Championship against Luke Woodhouse, a seasoned participant with a strong 2023 season. Expect a battle of wits and skill in this intriguing encounter.

Conclusion

As the World Darts Championship unfolds, each matchup promises to be a spectacle of skill, determination, and perhaps a few surprises. The Dutch contingent faces a challenging journey through the early rounds, and fans can anticipate nail-biting moments as these players strive for glory on the grand stage of Alexandra Palace.

Qualifying Process

Understanding how players qualify adds another layer of intrigue to the championship. World Darts Championship Format

The format evolves as the competition progresses. From best-of-five sets in the initial rounds to best-of-seven in the third and fourth rounds, the intensity builds. The quarterfinals shift to best-of-nine sets, followed by best-of-11 sets in the semifinals. The grand finale is a best-of-13 sets showdown, ensuring a thrilling climax to this prestigious tournament.

2024 World Darts Championship: Paddy Power’s ‘Big 180’ Campaign Scores for Prostate Cancer UK

December 3, 2023 by Herbert

The darting world is gearing up for a heartwarming twist at the 2024 World Darts Championship, courtesy of Paddy Power. In a surprising move, the Irish bookmakers have abandoned the idea of turning the treble 20 bed green, opting instead to donate £1,000 to Prostate Cancer UK for every 180 hit during the tournament. Let’s delve into the details of this ‘Big 180’ campaign and its potential impact on both the darts community and the fight against prostate cancer.

The Initial Jest: Treble 20 Turning Green?

Paddy Power initially sent shockwaves through the darting community by playfully announcing that the treble 20 bed would change from its traditional red color at Alexandra Palace. However, this quirky twist was revealed to be a cleverly orchestrated jest, setting the stage for a more profound and meaningful initiative.

The ‘Big 180’ Campaign Unveiled

Contrary to the lighthearted banter, the true intent of the ‘Big 180’ campaign is now unveiled. With 901 maximums hit during the previous World Darts Championship, Paddy Power aims to surpass this figure, pledging a potential £1 million donation to Prostate Cancer UK. The money raised throughout the championship, scheduled to be broadcast live on Sky Sports from December 15, will fund crucial research to diagnose men sooner and improve the lives of those affected by prostate cancer.

Collaboration for a Cause: Paddy Power and Professional Darts Corporation (PDC)

In a commendable joint effort, Paddy Power and the Professional Darts Corporation are set to leverage their channels, challenging 180,000 fans, followers, and customers in the UK to use Prostate Cancer UK’s online risk checker via prostatecanceruk.org/180-risk. This collaborative approach aims to raise awareness and encourage proactive measures against the most common cancer in men.

The Apology and the Purpose

Acknowledging the potential stress caused to players like Peter Wright and Michael van Gerwen, a Paddy Power spokesperson officially apologizes while emphasizing the greater cause. With one in eight men at risk of prostate cancer, the goal is to hit the bullseye with ‘The Big 180’ campaign, making it unmissable this Christmas and prompting men to take a mere 30 seconds to check their risk.

PDC’s Perspective: A Risk Worth Taking

Matthew Porter, the chief executive of the Professional Darts Corporation, reflects on the risk taken by Paddy Power in pranking players to garner public attention. However, the ultimate goal is clear — to encourage millions of viewers to take a brief moment during the World Darts Championship to assess their risk of prostate cancer.

Prostate Cancer UK’s CEO Speaks Out

Laura Kerby, chief executive of Prostate Cancer UK, expresses gratitude for becoming the official charity partner of the Paddy Power World Darts Championship. Positioned at the heart of the action, ‘The Big 180’ is poised to be a gamechanger, supporting men and their loved ones affected by prostate cancer, one maximum at a time.

A Champion’s Testimony: Dennis Priestley

Dennis Priestley, a two-time world champion who battled prostate cancer, joins the campaign. Having been diagnosed in November 2007 and successfully treated, Priestley becomes a living testament to the importance of early diagnosis. He asserts that nearly all cancers, including prostate cancer, can be beaten if caught early enough.

Players’ Pledge and Incentives

Peter Wright, the 2020 and 2022 PDC world champion, underscores the significance of knowing the risk of prostate cancer for men over 50. As players gear up for the championship, each maximum hit translates to a £1,000 donation to Prostate Cancer UK, providing an extra incentive to perform well on the darts stage.

Conclusion

In the spirit of the season and competitive darts, Paddy Power’s ‘Big 180’ campaign intertwines sportsmanship with a noble cause. The synergy between sponsors, players, and fans creates a unique platform to raise awareness about prostate cancer and contribute significantly to ongoing research and support initiatives.

FAQs

How can I contribute to the ‘Big 180’ campaign?

You can participate by following the challenge on Paddy Power’s channels and using the online risk checker on prostatecanceruk.org/180-risk.

Will the treble 20 actually turn green?

No, the initial announcement about changing the color of the treble 20 was a playful jest.

What happens to the donated funds?

All funds raised during the championship will go to Prostate Cancer UK for lifesaving research and support programs.

How can I watch the Paddy Power World Darts Championship?

The championship will be broadcast live on Sky Sports from December 15.

Why is Prostate Cancer UK’s risk checker important?

The online risk checker allows men to assess their risk of prostate cancer, promoting early detection and intervention.
